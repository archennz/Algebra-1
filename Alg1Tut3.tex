\begin{center}
{\bf Question 2: Abelian Groups of Order 12}
\end{center}

The cyclic group $(\mathbb{Z}/12\mathbb{Z})$ and the product groups $(\mathbb{Z}/3\mathbb{Z})\times(\mathbb{Z}/4\mathbb{Z})$ and $(\mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z})\times(\mathbb{Z}/6\mathbb{Z})$ all have order 12. Are any of these groups isomorphic?



\begin{center}
{\bf Question 3: Correspondence Theorem -- Cyclic Groups}
\end{center}

Let $G$ be the cyclic group of order 12 generated by $g$. Define a group homomorphism $\varphi:\mathbb{Z}\to G$ which maps $1$ to $g$. Verify that $\varphi$ is surjective. What is the exact correspondence given by the correspondence theorem?

{\bf Question 4: Extremely creative question title}
\end{center}

Let $G, G^\prime$ be groups with subgroups $H, H^\prime$. Show that $H\times H^\prime$ is a subgroup of $G\times G^\prime$. Furthermore, show that if $H$ and $H^\prime$ are both normal, then $H\times H^\prime$ is normal. Are all subgroups of $G\times G^\prime$ of the form $H\times H^\prime$ for some $H\subset G$, $H^\prime\subset G^\prime$?
