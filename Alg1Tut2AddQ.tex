 \noindent{\bf Exercise 1:}      
    Show that the canonical homomorphism
$\Z \rightarrow \Z / 5\Z$ does not preserves order of elements.\\

 \noindent{\bf Exercise 2:}  Suppose $\phi;G \rightarrow G'$ is a group homomorphism. 
 	Show that, for all $g \in G$, the order of $\phi(g)$ divides the order of $g$. 
 	Furthermore, prove that they are equal if $ \phi$ is injective.\\


 \noindent{\bf Exercise 3:} Prove that in any group, $ab$ is conjugate to $ba$.\\

 \noindent{\bf Exercise 4:} Prove that every subgroup of an abelian group is a normal subgroup.\\
 
 \noindent{\bf Exercise 5:} Show that if $A$ is a subgroup of $G$ and $B$ is a normal subgroup, then $AB = \{x \ | \ x =ab \ \text{for} \ a \in A, b \in B \}$ is a subgroup of $G$.\\
 
 \noindent{\bf Exercise 6:} An index 2 subgroup of a group is normal. (Third way to prove $A_n$ is normal!)\\

\noindent{\bf Exercise 7:} Show that if a group $G$ has just one subgroup $H$, then $H$ is normal.\\

 
 \noindent{\bf Exercise 8:} Show that any group of order 125 has a subgroup of order 5.\\
 
  \noindent{\bf Exercise 9:} Suppose $|G| = 25$. 
  If $G$ has only a subgroup of order 5, then $G$ is cyclic. \\
  
   \noindent{\bf Exercise 10:} Suppose $|G| = 55$. 
   Show that $G$ must have an element of order 5. \\
  
   \noindent{\bf Exercise 11:} Prove that every group of order $35$ has elements of order 5 and order 7.\\
   
   \noindent {\bf Exercise 12:} Prove that a group of even order must possesses at least one element of order 2.
